FRANCE

Paris

History

The history of Paris dates back to the Parisii tribe in the 3rd century BCE. During the 12th-century medieval period, Paris became the capital of the Kingdom of France and began constructing iconic Gothic architectures like Notre-Dame, establishing its status as a core European political and religious center.

Haussmann's Renovation of Paris

During the reign of Napoleon III in the 19th century, Baron Haussmann carried out a massive urban modernization project. He demolished narrow medieval streets to design grand boulevards, uniform white-stone buildings, and symmetrical blocks, shaping today's iconic romantic cityscape of Paris.

Culture

Café & Intellectual Culture

Paris's café culture is not just about dining, but the cradle of European thought and artistic movements. For centuries, countless philosophers, writers, and artists (such as Voltaire, Hemingway, Sartre) gathered in Left Bank cafés to debate and exchange ideas, fostering a profound humanistic spirit.

Haute Couture & Aesthetic Lifestyle

As the global capital of fashion, Paris weaves "beauty" into every detail of daily life. From Haute Couture fashion weeks and exquisite window displays to meticulous tastes in gastronomy, architecture, and attire, it embodies the French art of living (Art de Vivre).



Core Attractions

Eiffel Tower & The Seine River

Built to commemorate the centennial of the French Revolution, the Eiffel Tower is the most iconic landmark of Paris. The Seine River flowing through the city center and its riverbanks are designated as a UNESCO World Heritage site, offering classic views from a river cruise.

More

The Louvre Museum

The world's largest art museum and historic monument, featuring an iconic glass pyramid entrance that blends classical and modern architecture. It houses tens of thousands of world-class art masterpieces, including the Mona Lisa and the Venus de Milo.

More

Arc de Triomphe & Champs-Élysées

Commissioned by Napoleon to commemorate military victories, the Arc de Triomphe stands majestically at the center of Place de l'Étoile, connecting to the Champs-Élysées—hailed as the "world's most beautiful avenue"—lined with luxury boutiques and French romance.

More

Artistic Footprint

Explore the artistic hill of Montmartre and Shakespeare and Company bookstore, experiencing the inspirational havens where Impressionist painters and Lost Generation literary giants once resided.

Travel Tips

Recommended travel season is spring (April to June) or autumn (September to November); taking the Metro is very convenient, though visitors should remain vigilant of pickpockets.
