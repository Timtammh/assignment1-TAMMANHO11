Egypt

Giza&Cairo

History

Dating back about 4,500 years (26th century BCE), Pharaohs of the Fourth Dynasty chose the Giza Plateau as their final resting place. Mobilizing tens of thousands of artisans, they built the pyramids—a marvel of human architecture symbolizing the peak of royal and divine power.

Islamic Golden Age

Following historical evolution, Cairo became the political and intellectual center of the Islamic world after the 10th century CE. Notably, after Saladin established the Cairo Citadel in the 12th century, Cairo gradually developed into home to one of the world's densest concentrations of historic Islamic architecture.

Culture

The Afterlife Belief

Ancient Egyptians deeply believed in the existence of an afterlife, which led to the development of monumental tomb architecture, intricate mummification techniques, and the tradition of recording history through wall paintings and hieroglyphics.

Bazaar & Social Life

In the heart of Cairo, the Khan el-Khalili bazaar has been a gathering place for merchants and artisans for centuries. Locals enjoy sitting at street-side tea houses sipping tea or smoking shisha, embodying a slow-paced social lifestyle rich in Middle Eastern charm.

Giza Pyramids & The Great Sphinx

Comprising the Great Pyramid (Khufu), the Pyramid of Khafre, and the Pyramid of Menkaure, alongside the mysterious giant stone sculpture of the Great Sphinx. It is the only wonder of the ancient world still standing today.

Cairo Citadel & Mosque of Muhammad Ali

Perched high above the city, this Ottoman-style mosque features massive onion-shaped domes and soaring minarets, offering the best vantage point to overlook the entire Cairo skyline..

Grand Egyptian Museum

Housing countless rare treasures, including the complete set of golden artifacts excavated from the tomb of Tutankhamun. It is one of the most anticipated museum projects globally in modern times.

Local Cuisine

Introducing Egypt's national dish, Koshary—a unique staple made of macaroni, rice, lentils, crispy fried onions, and spicy tomato sauce.

Travel Tips

Recommended travel season is from October to April (avoiding the scorching desert summer). Modest attire is required when visiting mosques or traditional areas to respect local culture.
