Osaka

History

Osaka's history dates back to ancient times, having served as one of Japan's early capitals (Naniwa-kyo). With its advantageous geographic location, Osaka has long been a vital port and commercial hub connecting Japan to the Asian mainland, deeply influencing the nation's political and economic development.

Tenka no Daidokoro: Merchant Capital

During the Edo period, Osaka was known as the "Nation's Kitchen" (Tenka no Daidokoro). At the time, goods and grain from across the country converged here, giving rise to an active merchant class and a unique "townsman culture," laying the foundation for its modern commercial prosperity.

Culture

Kuidaore Food Culture & Warm Hospitality

Osaka boasts the famous "Kuidaore" food culture, meaning to eat oneself into bankruptcy. Osakans are warm, humorous, and straightforward, valuing human connection. This relaxed and cheerful street-level culture contrasts sharply with Tokyo's formal atmosphere.

Comedy Capital & Entertainment

Regarded as Japan's comedy capital (Owarai), Osaka has nurtured countless famous comedians and traditional comedic forms (such as Yoshimoto Kogyo's Manzai). Humor and laughter have deeply integrated into the daily life and soul of Osakans.



Core Attractions

Osaka Castle

Built by Toyotomi Hideyoshi in the 16th century, it is one of Japan's most famous castles, symbolizing power and unification. The majestic main keep is surrounded by moats and vast greenery, with its interior now serving as a history museum and the top floor offering panoramic views of Osaka.

More

Dotonbori & Shinsaibashi

Osaka's ultimate commercial and entertainment hub, featuring the iconic "Glico Running Man" giant neon sign along the Dotonbori canal. The surrounding Shinsaibashisuji shopping arcade brims with retail stores, pharmacies, and local street food, making it a must-visit to experience Osaka's neon-lit nightlife.

More

Universal Studios Japan - USJ

A world-class theme park bringing together ultra-popular movie and anime worlds, including "Super Nintendo World" and "The Wizarding World of Harry Potter," delivering an immersive and ultimate entertainment experience for visitors.

More

Local Street Food Delicacies

Introducing Osaka's world-famous soul foods, such as crispy-yet-creamy Takoyaki (octopus balls), golden-crisp Okonomiyaki (savory pancakes), and fragrant Kushikatsu (deep-fried skewers).

Travel Tips

Recommended travel seasons are spring (March to May for cherry blossoms) and autumn (September to November for fall foliage) for the most comfortable weather; public transport is extremely efficient, and using an ICOCA card or the Osaka Amazing Pass makes exploring attractions effortless.
